# Everything You Need to Know About the Microbiome

A clinical microbiology reference that **bridges the familiar Gram-stain/morphology scheme to modern phylogeny** and, on that base, **deconstructs the oversimplified dogmas** that came with the microbiome boom — using mechanism and evidence, not hype.

## Thesis / perspective

As next-generation sequencing (NGS) and metagenomics entered the clinic, understanding bacteria at the **phylum level** suddenly mattered — yet most clinicians were never taught this framework (we memorized Gram stain and morphology), while the microbiome boom spread oversimplified claims such as *"diversity = health"* and *"Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio = obesity."*

So this document does two things:

1. **Bridge** — it maps the familiar Gram/morphology four-class scheme (**GPAC / GPAB / GNAC / GNAB**) onto modern phylogeny (Bacillota, Bacteroidota, Actinomycetota, Pseudomonadota, …), so an NGS-era clinician can re-read bacteria by lineage and mechanism.
2. **Deconstruct** — on that base, it shows *where simple rules break down*, with mechanism and evidence: "metronidazole covers all anaerobes," "diversity = health," "in-vitro activity = C. diff prevention," "F/B ratio = obesity," and more.

It is **not a neutral encyclopedia but a mechanism-first, skeptical field guide to clinical microbiology.**

## Contents

**I. Classification reference (the backbone)**
- Phylum-level placement of clinically relevant bacteria; the four anaerobe classes (GPAC/GPAB/GNAC/GNAB) with representative species, diseases, contaminated foods, and antibiotic susceptibility
- Facultative anaerobes ("not-quite-anaerobes" that dominate clinical isolates)

**II. Clinical deep dives & dogma-deconstruction**
- Antibiotic exceptions by mechanism — where penicillin, metronidazole, fluoroquinolones, and macrolides fail (*Actinomyces*, *Aggregatibacter*, the "which drug works" halitosis puzzle)
- Halitosis: VSC-producing bacteria, substrate foods, and the three H₂S-generation pathways
- SIBO treatment; *C. difficile* risk in perspective; microbial drug metabolism (*Eggerthella*: digoxin, levodopa)
- Gut–brain axis: neurodegenerative and psychiatric disease
- Deconstructing "colon diversity = health" and the "F/B ratio = obesity" myth
